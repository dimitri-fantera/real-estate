# Y Combinator Companies Touching Real Estate

> Comprehensive scan of every YC batch (S05–W26) for companies in proptech, property management, mortgage tech, construction tech, commercial real estate, home buying/selling, rental platforms, and adjacent categories.
>
> Last updated: March 2026

---

## Overview

YC's directory lists approximately **70 Proptech**, **58 Housing & Real Estate**, **40 Construction**, and **121 Real Estate & Construction** startups (with overlap across categories). Real estate was virtually absent from YC before 2009 — Airbnb (W09) was the first. The sector picked up meaningfully around 2014 and has accelerated since 2022, with recent batches heavily AI-driven.

## Key Trends

1. **AI is eating proptech (2024–2026)**: Recent batches are overwhelmingly AI-first — AI agents for brokerage (Henry, PARES), property management (Avery, Brickwise, Haven), appraisals (Automax), lease analysis (Propaya), construction docs (Bild AI, Articulate, Opusense), and permitting (PermitFlow, PermitPortal).

2. **Construction tech is the deepest vertical**: From PlanGrid (W12) and EquipmentShare (W15) to PermitFlow (W22) and Scout Out (W26), construction has the longest sustained investment thread and YC's biggest pure RE exit (EquipmentShare).

3. **Global expansion**: Strong LatAm presence (QuintoAndar, Houm, Mudafy, GoJom, Philippo, Clau), Southeast Asia (Homebase), India (Landeed, Material Depot), and Europe (HOMLI, Brickwise, Hypcloud).

4. **W14 was the golden batch**: Produced three of YC's most valuable proptech companies — Snapdocs ($1.5B), QuintoAndar ($5.1B), and Guesty ($900M).

5. **Rent-to-own struggled**: Both Divvy Homes (S17) and ZeroDown (W19) were hit hard by rising interest rates post-2022.

6. **Many "YC proptech" companies aren't actually YC**: Opendoor, Flyhomes, Side, Pacaso, Ribbon, Orchard, Juniper Square — none confirmed as YC-funded, despite frequent association.
