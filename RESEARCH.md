# Research Roadmap

## What We've Done

1. **Persona Mapping** -- Mapped 67 distinct user personas across 12 categories in the real estate universe (see `USER_PERSONAS.md`)
2. **Initial Product Idea** -- Generated DealScope concept: AI underwriting platform for mid-market RE investors (see `PRODUCT_IDEA.md`)

## The Gap

We jumped to a product idea before doing the hard work of figuring out *which personas to build for*. The product idea should follow from the research, not precede it.

## Next Steps (In Order)

### Step 1: Pain Severity Ranking
Which personas have problems that are urgent, frequent, and expensive? A problem someone complains about on Reddit is different from one they'd pay $500/month to solve tomorrow.

- Score each persona cluster on: frequency of pain, cost of the problem, urgency, and current alternatives
- Goal: Narrow 67 personas down to 5-10 worth going deep on

### Step 2: Market Structure Analysis
For the most painful personas:
- How concentrated is the market? (Few large players vs. fragmented long tail)
- How do they buy software today? (Top-down vs. bottom-up, individual vs. company purchase)
- What's their willingness to pay? (And ability -- a maintenance tech has different budget authority than a fund manager)
- Are there natural aggregation points? (Associations, conferences, communities, marketplaces)

### Step 3: Competitive Moat Assessment
- Where is there entrenched software (Yardi, CoStar, RealPage) vs. genuine whitespace?
- Where would an AI-native approach create a step-change vs. just a nicer UI?
- What data or network effects could a new entrant build that incumbents can't easily replicate?

### Step 4: Founder-Market Fit Mapping
- Which segments reward technical founders vs. domain insiders?
- Where does YC's current thesis (AI-native, fast-growing, massive TAM) align best?
- What kind of team would be credible in each segment?

## Guiding Principles

- **Don't fall in love with the first idea** -- Let the research point to the opportunity
- **Pain over elegance** -- The best startup ideas often look unsexy; what matters is severity of the problem
- **Willingness to pay is the real signal** -- "This is annoying" ≠ "I would pay to fix this"
- **YC qualities**: Massive market, 10x better (not 10%), AI-native moat, fast growth potential, clear founder-market fit
